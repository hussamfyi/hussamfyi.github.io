--- layout: post title: Design is dying. Or is it? tags: product design ui/ux ---

I come across at least 1 rehashed spicy take on design every day while scrolling through Twitter.

  • Designers should learn how to code.
  • AI is going to replace designers in 5 years.
  • You shouldn't name your Figma layers.

Recently I came across yet another "UX is dead" tweet. This time from a seasoned designer (and now full-time creator) called Michal Malewicz.

UX is dead.

Paul sees that shift too.

And so many are still in denial. pic.twitter.com/plI8qMTs1A

— Michal Malewicz (@michalmalewicz) August 10, 2023

As someone learning UI/UX in my spare time, it can be disheartening to read takes like this. There may be a sprinkle of truth to them, but it's useful to remind yourself that people are incentivized to say controversial things on social media because it leads to clicks and eyeballs.

Still, I think it's worth considering how the UI/UX design field will evolve over time, especially with the advent of AI, and exploring what designers can do to adapt to our fast-changing world.

Mind you, I'm not a product designer, so I can't offer a credible perspective on this topic.

Instead, I'll share what other designers think by posting their takes here (from spicy to inspirational) and I'll add a comment to each one if I feel I can add to the conversation.

Grab some popcorn and enjoy.
